# Callverted Homepage — v8 Hybrid Spec (source of truth for /v8 build)

> This is the founder's synthesized hybrid recommendation. For the /v8 build, THIS document
> is the primary source of truth for copy and behavior. Where this doc is silent, fall back to
> `docs/landing-copy-v5-v6.md` (the locked template). Where the two conflict (a few reworded
> lines — hero subhead, See-It-Work headline/subhead, Jobs-vs-Messages closing line, the Math
> disclaimer), THIS doc wins.

## The direction

Build a **new hybrid version**, not just pick one unchanged. The strongest combination:

* **v6's dark split hero and overall story spine**
* **v7's more polished, enterprise-feeling visual system**
* **v5's restraint and readability**
* **a redesigned demo where each tab actually behaves differently**

The locked positioning and section order are already strong; do not rethink the whole strategy.
The product is still framed as lead recovery across missed calls, answered calls, and website
inquiries, ending in one ranked callback queue.

## Overall visual direction

Use a polished vertical-SaaS look rather than a playful AI startup look. The site should feel:
credible, operational, premium, built specifically for contractors, and visual enough to explain
the product without much reading.

Use the dark navy visual language from v6 and v7 for major proof moments, but avoid making the
entire page dark. **Alternate dark and light sections to create a clear rhythm.** Keep rounded
cards and subtle motion, but reduce decorative effects that do not explain the product. Use real
trade photography selectively — rotating/cross-fading photography can make the product feel more
established, but it should support the story, not become a generic slideshow.

## 3. The Leak
Three-card format. Headline: **There's more than one way to lose a job.** Support: **Every inbound
opportunity that goes uncaptured, undocumented, or unprioritized is a job your competitor can win.**
- Card 1 — **The call rings out.** Nobody is free, or it is after hours. The caller does not leave a
  voicemail and calls the next company. Chip: **Missed · on a job**
- Card 2 — **The details never get written down.** The call your team answered, or the web inquiry
  nobody worked, lives in someone's memory until it disappears. Chip: **Web lead · never worked**
- Card 3 — **The big job waits behind the small one.** Five callbacks sit in one queue with no way
  to distinguish the emergency from the tune-up. Chip: **Hot lead · called back 4th**

Keep cards visually strong but concise — understood in five seconds.

## 4. How It Works
Light section, four **connected** steps (line/arrow/progressive workflow — NOT four unrelated
feature cards). Headline: **Every way in. One ranked list out.** Support: **One system on your phone
line and website makes sure nothing that comes in gets lost.**
- Step 1 — **Your team gets the call first.** Every call rings your existing phones first.
- Step 2 — **Callverted captures the rest.** It handles missed and overflow calls, captures the
  calls your team answers, and collects website inquiries.
- Step 3 — **It sorts and scores.** Every intake becomes a qualified job, a documented message, or
  a screened non-lead. Jobs are scored by urgency and value.
- Step 4 — **Your team gets a ranked list.** The highest-priority job appears at the top with
  everything needed for the callback.

## 5. See It Work — THE CENTERPIECE
Larger, immersive treatment from v6, but redesign the three tabs so each demonstrates a genuinely
**different** intake experience. The right-side OUTPUT stays structurally identical across all tabs.

Headline: **Watch an opportunity become a ranked lead.**
Support: **The intake changes by channel. The result does not. Every real job becomes a
callback-ready packet and lands in the same ranked queue.**
Tabs: Missed call · Answered call · Website inquiry.
Fixed line under the tabs: **Different inputs. Same ranked output.**

### Tab 1: Missed Call — a live voice conversation
Left side (chat bubbles, alternating speakers; fields populate on the right as it progresses):
- Callverted: "Thanks for calling Blue Star. The team is out on a job, so I'll grab the details.
  What's going on?"
- Caller: "My water heater just burst. The basement is flooding."
- Callverted: "What ZIP are you in, and have you found the shutoff?"
- Caller: "33618. I can't find it."

Right side (output packet):
- Service: Water damage · Urgency: Emergency · ZIP: 33618 · Shutoff: Not located · Estimated value:
  $1.8k–$3.2k · Outcome: Job · Priority: Hot · Score: 92 · Action: Call back now.
Context badge: **Nobody picked up. Callverted answered in 14 seconds.**
Autoplay once, offer a replay button.

### Tab 2: Answered Call — materially different
Left side: a phone-call interface or waveform with a transcript between an EMPLOYEE and a caller.
Callverted is NOT a speaker; show a small status label: **Captured in the background**. Fields
appear while the team handles the call.
- Employee: "Blue Star, this is Mike."
- Caller: "Our AC quit and the house will not cool down. The system is about twelve years old."
- Employee: "I can get someone out this week. Let me take your details."

Right side (output packet):
- Service: HVAC repair · System age: 12 years · Timing: This week · Outcome: Job · Priority: Warm ·
  Score: 54 · Action: Follow up today.
Context badge: **Your team handled the call. Callverted captured and scored it automatically.**
(This proves Callverted adds value even when the call is answered.)

### Tab 3: Website Inquiry — NO transcript
Left side: a realistic website intake WIDGET. One or two selections happen automatically, then the
free-text is submitted.
- "What do you need help with?" → Emergency repair · New installation · Replacement · Estimate or quote
- "When do you need service?" → Immediately · Today · This week · Flexible
- "Where is the job?" → ZIP code input
- "Tell us what is going on" (free-text): "Need a quote to remodel a bathroom. Timeline is flexible."
- Button: **Send request**

Right side (output packet):
- Service: Bathroom remodel · Timeline: Flexible · Estimated value: $6k–$12k · Outcome: Job ·
  Priority: Warm · Action: Call within the hour.
Context badge: **Submitted through your website intake widget.**

The right-side output must remain structurally identical across all three tabs — that is how the
section visually demonstrates the product's architecture.

Under the interactive demo: the REAL dashboard screenshot (`/product/dashboard.png`) in a browser
frame. Caption: **Every opportunity lands in one place, highest priority first.**

## 6. Jobs vs Messages
Dark or lightly tinted section, two large output cards. Headline: **Not every call is a job.**
Subheadline: **Callverted knows the difference.**
- Job card — labels: qualified · scored · prioritized. Example: Water damage · Emergency · ZIP 33618
  · Estimated value $1.8k–$3.2k · Hot · Score 92 · Call back now.
- Message card — labels: documented · routed · no score. Example: Billing question · Customer name ·
  Callback requested · Concise summary · Does not enter the job pipeline.
Closing line: **A scored job or a tidy message. Never a pile of voicemails.**
Make the Job card visually stronger, but don't make the Message card look unimportant.

## 7. Hear It
Simple audio-player section. Headline: **Hear what your customer hears.** Support: **Press play to
hear the intake from start to finish. No hold music, no voicemail, and no phone menu.** Large
waveform player: caller waveform · Callverted waveform · elapsed time · transcript preview. A
rotating trade image can sit beside the player, but audio stays the focus. Until real audio exists,
clearly use a **sample** label rather than implying it is a real customer call.

## 8. Built for Your Trade
Borrow more heavily from v7's rotating photography and premium presentation. Headline: **It knows
what your trade's calls sound like.** Support: **The questions it asks and the way it scores change
by trade.** Large split layout — left: rotating/selectable trade list (Restoration · HVAC ·
Plumbing · Electrical); right: large trade photo · one realistic call scenario · trade-specific
details Callverted captures · a small sample priority result.

Example for HVAC (photo rotates to an HVAC technician):
Scenario: **"The AC is out, it is 91 degrees outside, and the system is twelve years old."**
Captured: no-cool · occupied home · system age · service timing · repair-versus-replacement signal.
Result: **Warm · 54 · Follow up today.**

This should feel more substantial/enterprise-ready than four identical small cards.

## 9. The Math
Clean calculator. Headline: **The leak is already costing you.** Support: **The question is not what
Callverted costs. It is what missed and mishandled opportunities are already costing you.**
Inputs: inbound opportunities per month · percentage currently lost or delayed · average job value.
Outputs: estimated monthly revenue leaking · recovered jobs needed to cover Callverted · potential
recovered revenue. Disclaimer: **This estimates the size of the opportunity. It does not assume
every lead becomes a booked job.** Avoid giant unrealistic numbers — use conservative defaults.

## 10. Pricing
One dominant pricing card (not multiple tiers). Headline: **One flat price. Everything included.**
Price: **$149/month** · Annual: **$1,499/year** · Trial: **14 days free**. Included: missed-call
intake · answered-call capture · website intake · qualification and scoring · ranked callback queue
· alerts · weekly reporting · guided setup. Beside the price card, a "We set it up with you" panel:
- We build the call flow and lead sources with you.
- You approve every service, price, and question.
- We run test calls together before launch.
- A real person helps you tune it after launch.
- No contracts.
- Cancel anytime.
- One recovered job can cover the cost.
Primary CTA: **Start recovering leads**.

## 11. FAQ
Keep the category-clarifying questions, tighten answers to ~2–3 sentences each. Questions:
Is this an AI receptionist? · Does it replace my phone number? · Does it handle website leads? ·
What happens to calls my team answers? · Can it answer questions or book appointments? · What
happens during an emergency? · How fast can we launch? (Use the locked-copy answers, tightened.)
The FAQ resolves objections; it does not carry the core explanation.

## 12. Closing CTA
Dark closing section echoing the hero. Headline: **Stop losing jobs.** Subheadline: **Give your
team the first ring. Callverted captures, sorts, and ranks the rest.** Primary CTA: **Start
recovering leads** · Secondary: **Book a 15-minute demo**. Trust line: **14-day free trial · your
team gets the call first · no per-lead fees**. Subtle floating lead alerts in the background, but
REDUCE the number — three realistic alerts feel more credible than six large fictional job values.

## Mobile behavior
Desktop demo can be immersive; mobile should be simpler. For each demo tab: (1) show the input
interface, (2) animate the interaction, (3) transition vertically into the result packet. Do NOT
place two tiny columns side by side on mobile. Trade section on mobile: horizontal trade selector ·
one large image · one scenario · one result card. Hero animation simplifies to one source card
transitioning into one ranked packet.

## Final build recommendation
Use v6 as the technical/structural starting point. Keep: dark split hero · alternating section
rhythm · large interactive proof section · trade photography · ranked-output visual language.
Replace: the three nearly identical demo tabs · overly dense section layouts · repetitive animated
lead cards · website inquiry presented as a conversation. Add from v7: rotating trade photography ·
refined image transitions · cleaner enterprise polish · stronger large-format photography. Add from
v5: lighter copy density · simpler supporting layouts · more breathing room around major messages.

The most important rebuild is the demo: keep the standardized OUTPUT while making the INPUTS visibly
different (live voice convo / background-captured answered call / real website widget form).
