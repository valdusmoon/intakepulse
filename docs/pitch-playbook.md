## The decision: wedge and umbrella

The wedge opens every cold channel. The umbrella is what prospects discover once they're in.

The missed-call angle wins the door, but only after one critical narrowing: never pitch "you miss
calls" (deniable, and mildly insulting to a competent operator). Pitch **the calls you physically
can't take**: under a house, on a roof, on the other line, driving, 9 PM, asleep. That's physics,
not incompetence, so there is nothing to object to. The airtight subset is after-hours: no owner
claims they answer at 2 AM, and in emergency trades that's exactly when the $4k–$15k calls come.

The pitch has exactly **one belief shift** to land, and everything hangs off it:

> **Voicemail is not a safety net. A customer with water on the floor doesn't leave a message;
> they call the next company on Google.**

Once that lands, "I answer my calls" stops being an objection, because the claim was never that
they don't answer. The claim is that the calls they *can't* answer currently go to a competitor,
and now they don't.

The umbrella (every channel captured, jobs vs messages, ranked callback list, speed-to-lead
tracking) is the **second beat**, delivered only after the wedge gets a nod. It answers "is that
all?", justifies the flat price, and is why they stay. It is never the opener for cold traffic.

The accountability question ("how many leads did you get last month, and how fast did they get a
callback?") is **not** the cold opener either. It asks an owner to admit ignorance before trust
exists. It's the demo / second-conversation weapon and the retention story.

### The spine (one sentence, all channels)

> **When you can't answer, Callverted picks up, captures the job, and has it on your phone before
> the customer can call your competitor.**

### The message ladder

| Channel | Leads with | Depth |
|---|---|---|
| Cold call | Wedge only (the can't-answer call) | Broaden only if the conversation continues |
| Ads (Meta/Google) | Wedge only + test-call CTA | One idea per ad |
| Ad landing page | Wedge hero | Umbrella body (future paid-traffic variant) |
| Homepage | Umbrella ("Stop losing jobs") | Already built; wedge is Leak card #1. No change |
| Demo / second call | Umbrella + accountability | Ranked list, jobs vs messages, callback-speed numbers |

### The proof strategy (honest about stage)

No testimonials, no case studies, so never imply scale. Two assets replace social proof:

1. **The test call IS the demo.** Test mode works before paying. Every pitch closes with "call
   this number and pretend your water heater burst" instead of "book a meeting." The prospect
   judges with their own ears in 60 seconds.
2. **Founder honesty.** "I built this" is an asset with solo trades operators; they respect it.
   Never pretend to be a big company.

Do not cite a made-up "X% of callers don't leave voicemail" stat anywhere. Phrase it as behavior
("callers don't leave voicemails, they call the next company") until a citable source is found.

## The cold call

Target: owner-operators, 1–5 trucks, plumbing / HVAC / restoration / electrical, who answer their
own phone. Swap the trade scene per vertical: burst water heater / AC out at 94° / water loss at
midnight / dead panel. (Seasonal note: in summer, lead HVAC calls with the no-cool scene.)

### Script

**Opener (they answer):**

> "Hey [name], it's Nile. Full disclosure, this is a cold call. Thirty seconds and you can tell me
> to get lost. Fair?"
>
> "You run [Company], mostly you and a small crew, right? So, quick question. It's two in the
> afternoon, you're under a house, and your phone rings. What happens to that call?"

Let them answer. Whatever they say (voicemail, call back later, partner grabs it), continue:

> "Right. Here's the ugly part: a customer with a real problem almost never leaves a voicemail.
> They hang up and call the next company on Google. The job's gone before you're back in the truck.
>
> That's what I fix. You keep your number, your phone still rings first, nothing changes when you
> answer. But when you can't, on a job, after hours, second line, it picks up, asks what happened,
> where, and how urgent, and the whole job is on your phone ten seconds after they hang up.
> Problem, ZIP, how bad it is, roughly what it's worth. You call back when your hands are free."

**Preempt the receptionist flinch (say it before they raise it):**

> "And it's not one of those AI receptionists. It doesn't chat, doesn't book, doesn't quote a
> price unless you wrote the price. Three questions, then it hands you the job."

**Close (the test call, never a meeting):**

> "Don't take my word for it. I'll text you a number. Call it when you get a minute and pretend
> your water heater burst. You hear exactly what your customer would hear. If it would've saved
> you even one job this year, it's $149 a month flat, first two weeks free, no contract.
> Sound fair?"

### Objection handling (agree, then reroute; never argue)

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I answer my calls." | "Good, then it never says a word. It only exists for the call you physically can't take. What's the plan at nine at night?" |
| "I've got voicemail." | "When did a new customer last leave you one? People with water on the floor don't leave voicemails. They call the next guy." |
| "My wife / office handles the phone." | "And when she's out, or it's Sunday night? It backs her up, doesn't replace her. The calls she answers get logged too, if you want that." |
| "AI sounds robotic." | "That's exactly why I want you to call the test number before deciding anything. Judge it with your own ears." |
| "Another subscription." | "One recovered [$2,500 water heater] job pays for over a year of it. If it never catches a job you'd have missed, cancel. No contract." |
| "I have an answering service." | "A service takes a message and charges per call. This captures the actual job: what, where, how urgent, what it's worth. Flat price. And it catches your website leads and ranks who to call back first." |
| "Send me some info." | "The info is the test number. I'll text it now. Call it, pretend your AC died, then tell me it's not for you." |

### Voicemail-as-demo (when THEY don't answer your cold call)

Their voicemail is your proof. Text:

> "Hey [name], just called and hit your voicemail. So do your customers, and most won't leave
> one. They call the next company. I built the fix. Call [demo number] and pretend your AC died,
> you'll hear it in 60 seconds. — Nile, Callverted"

## Ads

### Meta (interruption traffic; one scene per ad; always the test-call or trial CTA)

1. **Scene ad.** Photo: tech under a sink / on a roof. Headline: **"You were on a job. They called
   the next company."** Body: "Callverted picks up when you can't. It asks what happened, where,
   and how urgent, and the job's on your phone in ten seconds. Your phones always ring first.
   $149 flat, 14-day free trial."
2. **Alert-as-creative.** The actual push notification as the image: 🔥 Hot 92 · Water damage ·
   ZIP 33618 · est. $1.8k–$3.2k · Call back now. Headline: **"This is what a missed call looks
   like now."**
3. **After-hours.** Dark image, phone glowing. Headline: **"9:47 PM. Burst pipe. A $4,000 job. It
   went to whoever answered."** Body: "Callverted answers when you're off the clock and hands you
   the job ranked by urgency and value."

### Google Search (intent traffic; meet the category they typed, then reframe)

- Keywords: "answering service for plumbers/HVAC/contractors", "after hours answering service",
  "missed call text back", "AI receptionist for contractors".
- Headline pattern: **"Picks Up When You Can't"** / **"Not an Answering Service"** /
  **"$149 Flat, No Per-Call Fees"**.
- Description: "Your phones ring first. When nobody answers, AI intake captures what happened,
  where, and how urgent, then ranks your callback list. 14-day free trial."

## Field validation

This is validated on the phone, not in code: run the script on the first 20–30 cold calls, tally
which objections actually occur and where calls die, and track test-call completion rate (how many
prospects actually dial the demo number). The close depends on a clean test-call experience, so do
one full run-through of the demo number in test mode before the first call.
