# Callverted Homepage — v9 "Blunt Simplicity" Spec (source of truth for /v9)

> The lesson from Leaddi ("You Missed a Call. Leaddi Didn't."): the homepage can be mostly text
> because the product is a one-step mental model. We do NOT copy Leaddi's page — Callverted's
> positioning is broader (every inbound opportunity → ranked callback queue, not just missed-call
> SMS). What we STEAL is the **level of bluntness**. v9 keeps strong visuals but makes the copy
> hierarchy almost Leaddi-level direct: less big-SaaS storytelling, more blunt operator logic.
>
> This doc is the PRIMARY source of truth for /v9 copy. Use it verbatim.

## The whole page in four lines (copy hierarchy)
1. **Stop losing jobs.**
2. **Callverted captures every inbound opportunity.**
3. **It sorts jobs from messages.**
4. **It ranks who to call back first.**

Everything else (demo, trades, ROI, pricing) just supports that. When in doubt: shorter, blunter,
fewer words per line. Short declarative lines stacked vertically are the signature typographic move.

## Section spine (8 sections — leaner than v8; NO Hear-It, NO FAQ by design)
Hero → Problem → Mechanism → Demo → Differentiator → Trades → ROI → Pricing (+ a minimal closing CTA).

---

## 1. Hero
- Eyebrow: **Lead recovery for home-service trades**
- Headline: **Stop losing jobs.**
- Subhead (ONE sentence — this single line contains the whole product): **Callverted captures missed
  calls, answered calls, and website inquiries, then turns the real opportunities into a ranked
  callback list.**
- Primary CTA: **Start recovering leads** · Secondary: **Book a demo**
- Trust line: **14-day free trial · your team gets the call first · no per-lead fees**
- Visual: keep it lean — one restrained hero visual (e.g. a single ranked-job card, or a minimal
  "source → ranked packet" cue). Do not crowd the hero.

## 2. Problem
- Headline: **Good jobs are leaking before your team can call back.**
- Three short stacked lines:
  - Some calls get missed.
  - Some details never get written down.
  - Some high-value jobs wait behind low-value callbacks.
- Close: **Callverted catches the opportunity, documents the details, and shows your team who to
  call first.**

## 3. Mechanism
- Headline: **Many ways in. One ranked list out.**
- Three inputs (stacked / converging):
  - Missed call.
  - Answered call.
  - Website inquiry.
- Then: **Each one becomes a job, a message, or a screened non-lead.**
- Then: **Real jobs get scored by urgency and value, so the most important callback rises to the top.**
- Visual: a clean "3 in → 1 ranked out" diagram feel. Restrained, not busy.

## 4. Demo
- Headline: **See a lead get ranked.**
- Keep the redesigned THREE-tab demo (each tab behaves genuinely differently), but frame it with the
  simpler copy below:
  - **Missed call** — Callverted answers, asks the right questions, and captures the job.
  - **Answered call** — Your team talks. Callverted captures, summarizes, and scores in the background.
  - **Website inquiry** — The customer uses a short guided intake with multiple choice and one
    free-text answer.
- Same result every time: **Job packet · Hot / Warm / Cool · Recommended callback action.**
- Below the demo: the real dashboard screenshot (`/product/dashboard.png`) with a short caption
  (e.g. "Every opportunity, ranked in one place.").
- The three tabs reuse the same underlying scenarios as v8 (water-heater burst → Hot 92; HVAC no-cool,
  team answered → Warm 54; bathroom remodel website inquiry → Warm, no numeric score). The right-side
  output packet stays structurally identical across tabs.

## 5. Differentiator
- Headline: **Not every call is a job.**
- Three short contrasting lines:
  - A burst water heater is a job.
  - A billing question is a message.
  - A wrong number is screened.
- Close: **Callverted knows the difference.**

## 6. Trades  (keep this LEAN — a short blunt statement, not the big v8 showcase)
- Headline: **Built around the calls your trade actually gets.**
- Body: **Restoration, HVAC, plumbing, and electrical calls are not all the same. Callverted asks
  different questions and scores different signals based on the work you do.**
- Visual: a compact trade row / four tiles (restoration, hvac, plumbing, electrical). Don't over-build.

## 7. ROI
- Headline: **The leak is already costing you.**
- Body: **Estimate how many opportunities you lose or delay each month, and see how many recovered
  jobs it takes to cover Callverted.**
- 3-input calculator: inbound opportunities/mo · % lost or delayed · average job value → estimated
  revenue leaking · recovered jobs needed to cover Callverted ($149/mo) · potential recovered revenue.
  Conservative defaults; keep it honest (sizes the opportunity, does not promise every lead converts).

## 8. Pricing
- Headline: **One flat price. Everything included.**
- **$149/month.**
- **14-day free trial.**
- **No per-lead fees.**
- **No contracts.**
- **We set it up with you.**
- Primary CTA: **Start recovering leads**
- One dominant card, lean. Annual ($1,499/yr) may appear as a secondary toggle/line, but keep the
  emphasis on the single flat price.

## Closing CTA (minimal — the only thing added beyond the spine)
- Headline: **Stop losing jobs.** (bookends the hero)
- Primary: **Start recovering leads** · Secondary: **Book a demo**
- Trust line: **14-day free trial · your team gets the call first · no per-lead fees**
- Keep it to a single compact band. No floating-alert theatrics.

## Design direction
- NOT text-only like Leaddi (Callverted's workflow is more novel and needs the demo to show it), but
  the copy hierarchy is simpler and blunter than every other version.
- Distinct aesthetic from v5–v8: **predominantly light, high-contrast, typographic, lots of
  whitespace, one accent color.** Big bold headlines; short declarative stacked lines; thin rules;
  restrained decoration. This should read as "blunt operator logic," not "big SaaS storytelling."
- Give the page ONE strong visual anchor: the demo. It's fine to place the demo in a single dark
  accent band (the one dark moment on an otherwise light page) so it pops — everything else stays
  light and lean.
- Keep the nice visuals, but every section should be understandable in a few seconds of reading.
